Project Overview

This customer segmentation analysis leverages unsupervised machine learning algorithms to identify distinct behavioral patterns within a customer base of over 50,000 individuals. By applying K-means clustering and hierarchical clustering methods, we discovered five actionable customer segments, each with unique characteristics and business opportunities.

Methodology

  • Data collection and preprocessing (handling missing values, outliers, normalization)
  • Feature engineering: RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary value)
  • Dimensionality reduction using PCA for visualization
  • K-means clustering with elbow method for optimal cluster selection
  • Validation using silhouette scores and business logic
  • Segment profiling and actionable recommendation development

Technologies Used

Python, Scikit-learn, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly, Jupyter Notebook

Business Impact

Implementation of segment-specific marketing strategies resulted in a 34% increase in campaign effectiveness and a 22% improvement in customer retention rates. The VIP Champions segment alone contributed to 45% of total revenue despite representing only 18% of the customer base, validating the importance of targeted engagement strategies.

Key Insights

  • Purchase frequency is the strongest predictor of customer lifetime value
  • At-Risk customers show 60% recovery rate when engaged within 30 days of inactivity
  • Potential Loyalists convert to Loyal Customers at 40% rate with personalized offers
  • Seasonal patterns vary significantly across segments, requiring tailored timing strategies
